## Phase 1: Understanding Social Bonding Theory

### Step 1: Introduction to Social Bonding Theory

The social bonding theory, proposed by Travis Hirschi, suggests that delinquent behavior occurs when an individual's bond to society is weak or broken. **Community integration** plays a crucial role in strengthening these bonds through **community involvement**, which reduces the likelihood of reoffending. 
1. Understanding the elements of social bonding: attachment, commitment, involvement, and belief.
2. Recognizing how these elements are affected by an individual's experiences and environment.
3. Exploring how **community engagement programs for ex-offenders** can reinforce these bonds.
